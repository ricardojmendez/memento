About Memento

Memento is an experimental note-taking application for thoughts you may want to revisit in the future. It has some unique characteristics:

  • You can add notes at any time,
  • You can only edit or remove notes within a 24 hour window,
  • After that you can only follow up on them.

It was meant to capture my state of mind at a point in time, and not let me second-guess or fiddle with wording when some time had passed.

I'm also experimenting with spaced repetition reminders, so that Memento periodically brings up thoughts I don't want to forget, and allows me to examine the train of thought that lead me there.

If a thought is no longer as relevant as it used to be, you can archive it instead of deleting it. Archived thoughts are not included in searches by default, but you can also choose to search for them. You can un-archive a thought at any later point.

Memento is an open source project. You can check out the current issues and milestones on Gitlab.
